# The Nervous System Is Input: A Structural Account of Physiology, Cascades, and Professional Authority (v1.1)

## What this is
This is a short bridge artifact for professionals and educators working under pressure.

## Why it matters
It gives a simple boundary that can reduce escalation and preserve judgment:
**signals inform; they do not decide.**

## Core claim
The nervous system provides input. It does not authorize meaning or action.

Signals can be strong, accurate, urgent, and convincing.
They are still not authority.

## What this helps with
This document is for moments when:
- pressure rises quickly
- urgency starts to feel decisive
- early commitments become harder to reverse
- delay starts to look like weakness instead of professionalism

## What this is not
- not a diagnosis
- not therapy
- not a behavior plan
- not a neuroscience argument
- not a demand for agreement

## Core move
When pressure rises, preserve a brief interval between signal and commitment.

Useful questions:
1. What is my next first step?
2. What do I get for free?
3. Where is the boundary, and what dual behavior does it require?

## Who this is for
Professionals and educators who need a usable boundary under pressure, especially in classrooms and other fast-moving environments.
